# Knovia — 3-Minute Demo Script

> **Format:** Each section lists the **Action** (what to click/do on screen) and the **Script** (exact words to speak).
> **Total runtime:** ~3 minutes. Practice until smooth — each beat is timed.
> **Core message:** Lead with the PLATFORM, use SAT as one example domain.

---

## Setup (Before You Start)

- Run `npm run dev` and open the local Vite URL in browser.
- Navigate to the **Welcome Page** (home screen).
- Keep browser at full width so the side panel is visible.

---

## Beat 1 — Hook & Platform Positioning (0:00–0:15)

**Action:** Have the Welcome Page on screen. Point to the hero section, the three feature pills, and the course package cards.

**Script:**
> "Most learning tools are either chatbots or course players. Neither gives learners structure AND flexibility. Knovia is different — it is a structured learning platform that builds adaptive skill trees, surfaces rich visual content, and lets learners explore sideways without losing their place. Let me show you how it works."

---

## Beat 2 — Course Selection & Platform Flexibility (0:15–0:40)

**Action:** Point to the **three course packages** — Digital SAT Prep Sprint, Data Analyst Job Sprint, and the "Build Your Own" card. Highlight that each has a domain badge and stats.

**Script:**
> "This is the course library. Notice — same platform, different domains. SAT test prep, data analyst career sprint, or build your own from scratch. Every package brings its own skill tree, content types, and commands. The engine is domain-agnostic."

**Action:** Click **"Digital SAT Prep Sprint"**. The **Course Detail Page** appears with reviews, stats, and skill list.

**Script:**
> "Let's walk through the SAT package. Real learner reviews, a clear skill path, and a one-click start. This is how a creator ships a course on Knovia."

**Action:** Click **"Start Course"**. The **Setup / Intake Page** appears.

---

## Beat 3 — Personalization & Planning (0:40–1:00)

**Script:**
> "First, the platform personalizes. It asks for your starting point, your goal, your test date, and your weakest areas. Every learner gets a different plan."

**Action:** Click **"Fill Sample Inputs"**, then click **"Start Learning"**.

**Action:** Click **"Break 1400"** to advance the first planning step. Click through the remaining quick actions to complete planning.

**Script:**
> "The planning conversation asks targeted questions — not generic ones. The system builds an 8-week plan that front-loads the biggest scoring opportunity."

---

## Beat 4 — Skill Tree: The Visual Centerpiece (1:00–1:30)

**Action:** Point to the **skill progress bar** at the top. Note that **two skills** start in parallel. Point to the **Skill Tree tab** on the right panel — the graph with colored nodes and curved connections.

**Script:**
> "This is the skill tree — the platform's core. Skills unlock through dependencies, not just sequence. Reading & Writing and Algebra start in parallel, just like the real test. They converge into Adaptive Test Strategy. Every node is a separate learning workspace."

**Action:** Point to the **progress bar** in the skill tree sidebar showing "0/7 skills". Point to the **animated edges** connecting nodes.

**Script:**
> "The graph is live — curved connections show relationships, and each node type has its own color. Purple for topics, cyan for questions, orange for explanations, green for practice."

**Action:** Click **"Reading & Writing"** to open it. The **Content panel** auto-opens with a comparison table and metric strip.

**Script:**
> "Click a skill and look what loaded — a comparison table showing domain accuracy, and metric cards diagnosing exactly where the 220-point gap lives. Notice the content type badge at the top. These are structured content blocks, not chat text."

---

## Beat 5 — Chat, Content Resolution & Branching (1:30–2:00)

**Action:** Type in the chat: *"How do I tell the difference between an answer that is true and one that actually answers the question?"* Wait for the typing indicator and response.

**Script:**
> "I'll ask a real question. Watch — the typing indicator shows the system is processing. The response gives a structured reasoning process."

**Action:** Point to the **system message** showing "Content matched: ..." if it appears.

**Script:**
> "See that? The platform detected the intent and matched it to a content pack. That is not a keyword lookup — it is intent resolution running behind every message."

**Action:** **Select a portion of the assistant's reply text** with your mouse. Click **"Explore This"** in the popover.

**Script:**
> "Now watch — I select part of the answer and click 'Explore This.' The platform creates a side exploration branch. My main thread stays clean while I go deep on this concept. Look at the skill tree — a new node appeared, connected to the topic."

---

## Beat 6 — Creator-Defined Slash Commands (2:00–2:15)

**Action:** Click **"Reading & Writing"** in the skill bar to return. Click the chat input and type **`/`**. The **command autocomplete popover** appears above the input showing available commands.

**Script:**
> "Besides natural language, course creators define slash commands — structured actions tailored to their domain. I type slash and see the commands this SAT package ships with."

**Action:** Point to the popover showing the three commands: `/sat-drill`, `/strategy-cards`, `/diagnose`. Click **`/diagnose · Error Diagnosis`**.

**Script:**
> "Three commands: drill generation, strategy cards, and error diagnosis. Each one is creator-defined — different courses ship different commands. Let me run a diagnosis."

**Action:** The **command input dialog** opens. Type **"Algebra"** in the domain field. Click **"Run Command"**.

**Script:**
> "It asks for the domain — this is a structured form, not a free-text guess. I pick Algebra and run it."

**Action:** Point to the **command execution card** in chat (collapsible with lightning bolt icon). Point to the **Content panel** which loads an error analysis trace.

**Script:**
> "The command card shows exactly what was executed. And the content panel loads a reasoning error trace — structured diagnostic output, not a wall of text. Creators control the entire interaction model."

---

## Beat 7 — Content Types & Skill Completion (2:15–2:40)

**Action:** Type *"Show me SAT strategy flashcards"*. The **Content panel** updates with an **interactive flashcard deck**.

**Script:**
> "Now I ask for strategy cards — and the content panel updates with an interactive flashcard deck. But these aren't just flip cards. Watch — this first one is a classic flip card. Tap to reveal the answer."

**Action:** Click the flashcard to flip it. Then click **Next** to advance to card 2.

**Script:**
> "Card two is a fill-in-the-blank. I type my answer, hit Check — instant feedback. Next card — multiple choice with four options. I pick one and see green or red immediately. And card four — put these steps in the right order, then check. Four different interactive card types in one deck: flip, fill-blank, multiple choice, and ordering. Each one is purpose-built for how that concept should be practiced."

**Action:** Quickly demonstrate each card type: type an answer in fill-blank, pick an option in MCQ, tap items in order card. Click **"Complete"** next to Reading & Writing in the skill bar.

**Script:**
> "Mark it complete — and watch the cascade."

**Action:** Point to the skill bar — Reading & Writing shows a checkmark, **English Conventions** changes from Locked to Ready. Click **"English Conventions"** to enter it.

**Script:**
> "English Conventions unlocks — their weakest domain. Click in and look: a callout stack with color-coded reasoning traps. Anti-patterns in red, warnings in amber, tips in green, insights in blue. Every skill surfaces different structured content."

---

## Beat 8 — Inbox & Proactive Recommendations (2:40–2:50)

**Action:** Click the **Inbox button** (bottom-right). The popover opens showing suggested next actions with icons.

**Script:**
> "The platform doesn't just respond — it recommends. The inbox surfaces proactive suggestions: start your next skill, practice what you just learned, explore a related topic. Each recommendation is tied to your progress and behavior."

**Action:** Point to one or two inbox items showing the action icons and labels.

---

## Beat 9 — Close (2:50–3:00)

**Action:** Stay on the current view. Gesture to the full interface.

**Script:**
> "That is Knovia — structured skill trees, rich visual content, side explorations, creator-defined commands, intent-aware content matching, and proactive recommendations. One platform. Any subject. Not a chatbot. Not a course player. A structured learning system that adapts to how you think."
